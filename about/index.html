Dedicated to excellence.

Northeastern University's Student Value Fund is a student organization established in 2005 which currently has 25 active members.

The Fund is based around a student run portfolio that develops and maintains a long-only value investment strategy in United States mid to large cap equities. Currently, The Fund manages $150,000 in assets. Our goal is to create an environment that provides opportunies to establish a comprehensive understanding of the financial industry and ultimately produces well-rounded individuals equipped with the tools to add value in today's society.

Investment Philosophy

The fund seeks to outperform its benchmark, the Russell 3000 Index, primarily through stock selection alpha. This will be achieved by investing in equity securities trading meaningfully below their intrinsic value. SVF will analyze potential investments through a fundamental valuation process including a discounted cash flow analysis, comparables analysis, and HOLT valuation analysis.

SVF aims to buy and hold these investments until they reach their intrinsic value, regardless of the time horizon. The fund’s agnostic approach to holding periods will help minimize portfolio turnover and reduce portfolio expenses. The fund will be constructed with a bottom-up basis strategy consisting of approximately 15 equity investments across various sectors, market caps, and styles with the U.S. market in order to minimize idiosyncratic risk.

Educational Philosophy

SVF was introduced to Northeastern University as a mechanism of extracurricular and hands on education. The fund's most important goals include to promote greater knowledge about financial markets and to provide the opportunity for NU students to be able to participate in a professional and dynamic environment. We strongly believe in fostering a learning environment by providing students, regardless of their major, with application and lively discussion . In a continually globalized and competitive financial industry, we think that every student should always be looking for opportunities of active engagement in order to be more successful in the future. SVF will always work with the foremost goal of improving the education attained by NU students.

History

The Student Value Fund was created under the guidance of Professor Paul Bolster, Jim Madigan in his prior role as director of development for the D’Amore-McKim School of Business, and the late Dean Thomas Moore. The fund’s key benefactors include several alumni, as well as a matching contribution from the D’Amore-McKim School of Business. Two of these alumni, Jason Vaillancourt of Fidelity Investments and Dennis Ruggere, serve as the advisory board for the fund.

Timeline of Presidents

The Fund began operations in the fall of 2006 under its first student portfolio manager, Robert Mandeville. Since then, it has had several portfolio managers, including Allison Kenna (2007-2008), William Kirchner (2008-2009), William Mallon (2009-2010), Jay Byoun (Fall 2012), Kevin Afable (Spring 2013), and Bowen Li (2013-2014). The current president is Connor Flood.
