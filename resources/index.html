A Wealth of Information.


Being a member of the Student Value Fund has its perks.

Every analyst gets uninhibited access to a Dropbox account with years of market research, custom spreadsheets, and hard-to-come-by investment articles and literature.
Analysts also enjoy the opportunity to become involved in the community and receive mentorship from talented students and advisors. The club participates in the Internal Business Case Competition, NUCAIS, and the Economics Society Symposium, among other events.
Senior analysts enjoy access to Credit Suisse's Holt Web Application. The senior members use the application for valuation purposes and control the other analysts' valuations.
Finally, the Fund provides members with an opportunity for networking. Part of our mission is to build strong relationships within the club. Because members of the club are very driven and successful (just look to our alumni list), these connections are extremely valuable.

In-House Screening Software

Our latest initiative is to automate parts of the screening and valuation process using software. The current version of some of our applications can be downloaded here.
DataHunter
A JVM library for querying all the stock market data one could want, from option prices to analyst expectations to price earnings.
EasyDCF
A desktop application designed to return the intrinsic value calculated based on analyst expecations and user-inputted assumptions. Can run on its own or be tweaked for sensitivity analysis - brilliant!
We are actively recruiting technical talent to expand our software engineering capacities. For those interested in learning about the mathematics of valuation, adaptive software, and statistics, this is the opportunity for you.

Partnering With The Best

Members of the student value fund also enjoy heightened opportunities to become involved with some of our partner organizations on campus. These include, the Finance and Investment Club and CAIS, the Collegiate Alternative Investment Summit. Please visit their websites as well.
